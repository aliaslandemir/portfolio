Who am I?

I am a PhD candidate working in the fields of Biophysics and Biomineralization at the University of Oslo. My research focuses on stem cell differentiation, bone formation in hydrogels, and microfluidic technologies. I also research cell behavior on micropatterned substrates. I am passionate about microscopy, data analysis, and their applications in personalized medicine.

Areas of Interest

Biomineralization: Researching calcium phosphate transformations for bone tissue engineering.

Biophysics: Understanding how mechanical forces and biological interactions influence cellular behavior.

Organ-on-a-chip: Developing systems to mimic cellular environments for tissue engineering research.

Deep Learning: Applying AI models for automated image analysis and data classification.

Research & Expertise

  • PhD Candidate | Biophysics and Biomineralization - University of Oslo (Aug 2022 - Present)
    • Modeling of biomineralization to understand factors affecting bone regeneration
    • Experimental investigation of calcium phosphate biomineralization and interaction of bone/stem cells with the extracellular matrix
    • Developing and maintaining automation protocols and ensuring lab safety compliance
  • Visiting Researcher - University of Minnesota (Jul 2024 - Aug 2024)
    • Worked on a Norwegian Centennial Chair (NOCC)-funded project on engineering cell tissue through topological patterning
    • Designed and microfabricated topological defect patterns for tissue ordering
    • Investigated HUASMC cell mechanics on fibronectin micropatterns using Traction Force Microscopy and PIV analysis
  • Research Assistant - İzmir Institute of Technology (Mar 2020 - Aug 2022)
    • Detection of rare cells using deep learning and lensless holography
    • Assisted in the Applied Optics and Photonics Laboratory with a focus on diffraction patterns
  • Erasmus Intern - Umeå University (Aug 2019 - Oct 2019)
    • Characterization and analysis of bacterial p-pili folding-unfolding using Force Measuring Optical Tweezers
