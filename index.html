About

A quick introduction to my background.

Who am I?

I am a PhD candidate working at the interface of Biophysics and Biomineralization at the University of Oslo. My current research focuses on stem cell differentiation, bone formation in hydrogels, and microfluidic technologies. I also investigate cell behavior on micropatterned substrates, with ongoing interest in deep learning-enabled microscopy.

Areas of Interest

Biomineralization: Investigating calcium phosphate transformations for bone tissue engineering.

Biophysics: Investigating how mechanical forces & biological cues influence cellular behavior.

Organ-on-a-chip: Using lab-on-a-chip systems to mimic physiological conditions for tissue engineering.

Deep Learning: Using AI for data analysis.
